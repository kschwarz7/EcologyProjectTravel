About Page

On this trip you get to experience a lot. I got to take part in one of their student based travel programs in Baja, Mexico. We got to have hands on experience with scientists and locals to collect data and help in local conservation efforts.

Why should you travel with EPI?

Learn Through Expeditions Field Sites and Remote Locations are essential parts of their program. In field-based education there are learning oppourtunities around every corner.
Scientist Partners EPI partners with world-class scientists and scientific institutions at all of its program sites, but students aren't here for lectures. They'll assist in on-going research on critically threatened wildlife and ecosystems.
Engaging Communities At EPI, all education, even science education, takes place within a cultural context. Cultural exchange is an essential element of their programs. We were able to learn from one another throughout the whole trip.
Their Mission EPI's mission is to inspire youth with nature and empower them with science, fostering tomorrow's leaders. We envision a diverse movement building communities that cherish and restore the ecosystems that sustain them.
Their Model EPI is structured for sustainability. They draw on tuition dollars from Travel Programs, the generosity of donors, and the efforts of scientists. Not only this but they run programs that address environmental issues today while developing environmental leaders for tomorrow.
Their Method All of EPI's education programs utilize conservation projects, partnerships with scientists, and engagement with local communities to teach three key components.
